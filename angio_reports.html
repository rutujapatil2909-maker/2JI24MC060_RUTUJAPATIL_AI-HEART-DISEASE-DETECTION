Coronary Angiography Findings

Dominance: Right dominant system.

Left Main Stem (LMS): The left main stem appears normal with no significant disease. Ostium and shaft are widely patent.

Left Anterior Descending Artery (LAD): There is 85% stenosis in the proximal LAD with significant calcification noted on fluoroscopy. The mid LAD shows 70% narrowing with eccentric plaque. Distal LAD is patent with TIMI-3 flow.

Left Circumflex Artery (LCx): The proximal LCx demonstrates 75% stenosis. The first obtuse marginal (OM1) shows 60% narrowing consistent with moderate disease. Distal LCx is patent.

Right Coronary Artery (RCA): The proximal RCA has 90% stenosis with ulcerated plaque. The mid RCA shows total occlusion consistent with a chronic total occlusion (CTO) — no antegrade flow. The posterior descending artery (PDA) fills via left-to-right collaterals. Distal RCA shows 65% stenosis.
